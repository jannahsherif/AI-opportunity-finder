# Opportunity Finder — User Guide

**Live site:** https://ai-opportunity-finder-five.vercel.app

This is a personal tool built by Jannah Abdelhay. It uses an AI agent to search the internet in real time and surface hackathons, competitions, grants, and other opportunities that match what you are looking for. Every result comes with a direct link so you can apply straight away.

---

## The Two Pages

**About (index.html)**
This is the homepage. It gives a short introduction to  me (Jannah), my background in computer science and security, my experience, awards, and contact details. The LinkedIn profile and email address are both linked directly.

**Opportunity Finder (finder.html)**
This is the main tool. You describe what you are looking for in plain language, and the AI searches the web and returns a ranked list of real, currently open opportunities.

## How to Use the Finder

### 1. Type what you are looking for

Use the chat input at the bottom of the page. Write naturally, as if you were asking a person. Some examples:

- "Find me vibe coding hackathons online this month"
- "AI competitions with cash prizes open right now"
- "Cybersecurity CTF competitions coming up soon"
- "Math competitions for undergraduates"
- "Open source bounties for beginners"

You can also click any of the suggestion chips on the empty screen to start with a pre-filled search.

Press **Enter** or click the **Search** button to run the search.

### 2. Set your filters before searching

The sidebar on the left has two groups of filters. These are applied to your search, so set them before you hit Search.

**Filters (toggle on or off by clicking):**

| Filter | What it does |
|---|---|
| Remote only | Only returns opportunities you can participate in online |
| Free to enter | Excludes anything with an entry fee |
| Team-friendly | Prioritises opportunities that allow or encourage teams |
| Cash prize | Focuses on competitions that offer monetary prizes |
| Beginner-friendly | Surfaces opportunities suited to people newer to a field |

Highlighted chips are active. Click a chip to turn it on or off.

**Deadline window (select one):**

| Option | What it means |
|---|---|
| Within 2 weeks | Only shows opportunities closing in the next 14 days |
| Within 1 month | Opportunities closing within 30 days |
| Within 3 months | A wider window for planning ahead |
| Any deadline | No deadline filter applied |

### 3. Read the results

Results appear below the chat area. Each card shows:

- The name of the opportunity
- A short description covering what it is, the prize or award, and who it is for
- A deadline label showing the closing date and how many days remain
- Tags summarising the key attributes
- An **Apply** button that links directly to the source page

The left border of each card is colour-coded by urgency:

- **Red** means fewer than 10 days left
- **Amber** means between 10 and 30 days left
- **Green** means more than 30 days left

### 4. Refine your results

After your first search you can keep chatting. Ask follow-up questions to narrow things down:

- "Show me only solo-friendly ones"
- "Which of those have the biggest prize?"
- "Find more like the first one but with a longer deadline"

The AI remembers the context of the conversation so you do not need to repeat yourself.

---

## Tips for Better Results

**Be specific about the type.** Saying "vibe coding hackathon" gives sharper results than "coding competition."

**Mention your level.** If you are a student or new to an area, say so. The AI will factor that in when searching.

**Adjust the deadline window if results are thin.** If you search within 2 weeks and get nothing, switch to 1 month or 3 months and search again.

**Check the Apply link before the deadline.** The AI searches live data but deadlines can change. Always verify on the official page before applying.
